Modifications by Lau Daviña, 2026.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

1 April 2026 — Version 1.0

INITIAL RELEASE

Vinculeis Sans emerges from a desire to create subtle shifts in tone, rhythm and relation between characters, exploring typography as a field of connection.

The modifications focus on small but meaningful interventions:

• Terminals  
Terminals have been redrawn to soften or slightly extend their endings, introducing a sense of openness and continuity. In some cases, cuts become less abrupt, suggesting a gesture that does not fully close.

• Descenders  
Descenders have been adjusted to alter vertical rhythm and balance. Some characters extend further down, creating a more elastic baseline interaction and emphasizing difference within repetition.

• Weight expansion — Black  
A Black weight has been added to the family, introducing a denser and more assertive presence. This expansion explores contrast not only in thickness, but in how characters occupy space, relate to each other, and produce rhythm at larger visual intensities.

• Glyph adjustments  
Selected glyphs were refined to reinforce internal coherence after the changes in terminals, descenders, and weight expansion, maintaining legibility while allowing for small deviations.

• Expanded character set  
New glyphs have been added, including additional characters, symbols and emojis. These elements extend the expressive range of the typeface, introducing informal, affective and non-verbal layers into the typographic system.

Rather than a complete redesign, Vinculeis Sans operates through proximity and deviation — maintaining the structure of the original while introducing micro-transformations that affect how the text is perceived and felt.
